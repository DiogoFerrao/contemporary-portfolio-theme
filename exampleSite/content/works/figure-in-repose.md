A large-format charcoal drawing exploring stillness and the weight of the resting body. The tall, narrow composition emphasizes the vertical pull of gravity against the horizontal calm of the figure.

## Process

Drawn from life over three consecutive sittings, each lasting around four hours. The model held the same pose across all sessions, though subtle shifts in posture became part of the drawing — traces of movement within apparent stillness.

The charcoal was applied in layers, starting with light vine charcoal for the initial gesture and building toward compressed charcoal for the darkest passages. Selective erasing was used to pull light back out of the tonal ground.

## Materials

- Vine and compressed charcoal
- Kneaded eraser and chamois
- Fabriano Artistico paper, 300gsm

## Dimensions

70 × 105 cm

> "The body at rest is never truly still — it breathes, it settles, it holds the memory of every movement that came before."
