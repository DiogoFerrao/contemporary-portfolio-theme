A quiet study of how light enters a room and transforms the objects it touches. The near-square format was chosen to echo the shape of the window that inspired the piece.

## Concept

Most paintings of interiors focus on the furniture, the architecture, the human presence. This piece removes all of that, leaving only the light itself — the way it pools on a bare floor, catches the edge of a wall, dissolves into shadow.

## Process

Painted from direct observation over a series of mornings, always at the same hour. The canvas was returned to the same position each day so the light would fall identically across the surface. Small shifts in weather and season are recorded in the subtle variations of warmth across the composition.

> "I am not painting the room. I am painting what the room holds when no one is looking."

## Technical Notes

- 90 × 100 cm, oil on cotton canvas
- Painted over 14 sessions across three weeks
- The palette was limited to Naples yellow, raw umber, and titanium white
