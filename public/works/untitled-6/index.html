Untitled VI

2022 Watercolor

A study in transparency and flow, this watercolor piece explores how pigment settles into the texture of cold-pressed paper.

About This Piece

Working wet-on-wet, I allowed the colors to bleed and merge organically, guiding the composition with minimal intervention. The result captures a sense of spontaneity that is difficult to achieve with more controlled media.

The palette was intentionally restrained — just three pigments mixed directly on the paper surface.

Process

  1. Soaked the paper for ten minutes before stretching it on a board
  2. Applied a base wash of diluted yellow ochre
  3. Dropped concentrated pigment into the wet surface and tilted the board to direct the flow
  4. Allowed the piece to dry naturally over several hours without any heat assistance

The unpredictability of watercolor is what draws me back to it again and again. Each piece is a conversation between intention and accident.
