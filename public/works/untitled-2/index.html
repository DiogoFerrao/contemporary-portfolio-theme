Untitled II

2024 Acrylic on paper

A study in texture and layering, this piece explores the interplay between translucent washes and opaque mark-making.

Process

The work began as an experiment with fluid acrylics on heavyweight watercolor paper. Multiple layers were built up over several sessions, allowing each to dry fully before the next application.

Key techniques used:

  • Wet-on-wet washes for the background gradients
  • Dry brush strokes for the textural foreground elements
  • Palette knife work for the thicker impasto sections

Inspiration

This piece was inspired by the weathered surfaces found in old Lisbon — peeling paint, crumbling plaster, and the way time leaves its mark on the built environment.

“Every wall tells a story if you look closely enough.”

The color palette was drawn directly from photographs taken during morning walks through Alfama.

Exhibition History

This work was first shown at the 2024 Spring Group Exhibition at Galeria Municipal.
