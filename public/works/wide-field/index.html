Wide Field

2024 Oil on linen

A horizontal composition exploring the relationship between vast open space and the edges that contain it. The wide format was chosen deliberately to echo the experience of peripheral vision.

Concept

This piece grew out of a fascination with how we perceive landscape — not as a framed rectangle, but as an expansive, borderless field. The elongated canvas format forces the viewer’s eye to travel laterally, mimicking the slow scan of a horizon line.

Process

Worked wet-on-wet over three consecutive days to maintain a continuous surface quality. The linen was left unprimed at the edges, allowing the raw material to participate in the composition.

“The frame is not a boundary — it’s a suggestion.”

Technical Notes

  • 160 × 85 cm, oil on unprimed Belgian linen
  • Completed in a single extended session across three days
  • The palette was mixed entirely from earth pigments and zinc white
