A panoramic study of the Alentejo plains at midday, when the heat flattens everything into bands of color. The extreme horizontal format mirrors the experience of standing in that landscape — the horizon line stretching beyond peripheral vision.

## Concept

This piece belongs to an ongoing series examining how wide-format compositions alter the viewer's relationship to depicted space. By pushing the canvas to a 2.5:1 ratio, the eye is forced to travel laterally rather than settling on a central focal point.

The result is closer to how we actually experience open landscape — not as a framed scene, but as an unfolding peripheral field.

## Process

Painted on site over three consecutive days, then refined in the studio. The linen was left unprimed at the edges, allowing raw fabric to bleed into the painted surface and blur the boundary between image and object.
