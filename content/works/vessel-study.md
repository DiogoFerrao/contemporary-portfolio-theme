A hand-built ceramic form exploring the tension between functional object and sculptural presence. The vessel is deliberately too tall, too narrow — it resists use, insisting instead on being looked at.

## Process

Built using the coil method over three sessions, each separated by a day of slow drying. The surface was left unglazed except for a single band of ash glaze near the rim, creating a dialogue between raw and finished.

The firing was done in a wood-burning kiln over 36 hours. The unpredictable atmosphere of the kiln left subtle variations in tone across the surface — warm blush on the side facing the flame, cool grey on the sheltered side.

## Dimensions

48 × 14 × 14 cm
