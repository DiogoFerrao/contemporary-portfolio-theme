A wide-format painting exploring the dissolving boundary between sky and land. The horizontal emphasis forces the eye to travel slowly across the surface, encountering subtle shifts in temperature and saturation.

## Concept

The horizon line has always been a subject of fascination — it is the one line in nature that exists purely as a perceptual phenomenon. You can never reach it, never touch it. This piece attempts to paint that impossibility.

## Process

Working on an unusually wide linen panel, I built the surface over three weeks using thin glazes of oil. Each layer was mixed on the palette to be only slightly different from the one beneath, creating a gradient so gradual it becomes difficult to identify where one color ends and another begins.

The final surface has over forty individual layers.

## Dimensions

180 × 75 cm

## Notes

- Exhibited as part of *Liminal Spaces*, Culturgest, Lisbon, 2024
- The piece is intended to be hung at eye level so the painted horizon aligns with the viewer's natural line of sight
