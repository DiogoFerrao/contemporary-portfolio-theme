A wide-format painting that demands to be read left to right, like a sentence. The panoramic composition stretches the viewer's gaze across a continuous field of muted color, resisting the urge to settle on a single focal point.

## Concept

This piece is part of an ongoing investigation into peripheral vision and the way we absorb landscape not as a framed moment but as a slow lateral scan. The proportions — nearly 3:1 — were chosen to push against the conventions of easel painting.

## Process

Worked wet-on-wet over two consecutive days on a single unbroken linen panel. The palette was mixed entirely in advance: five tones of grey-rose and warm stone, applied with wide house-painting brushes to eliminate any trace of fine detail.

The result is deliberately anti-photographic — there is no horizon line, no figure, no depth. Just color moving across a surface.

## Dimensions

200 × 80 cm

## Notes

- First shown at *Linha Longa*, Galeria Avenida, Lisbon, 2024
- The title refers both to the format and to the Greek *pan horama* — "all that is seen"
